## Why Gold Equities Deserve Your Attention

While physical gold and gold ETFs get most of the spotlight, **gold mining equities** offer something unique: leveraged exposure to gold prices combined with the potential for dividends and operational improvements.

But how do you actually compare one gold stock to another?

### Key Valuation Metrics

#### 1. All-In Sustaining Cost (AISC)

The AISC tells you how much it costs a company to produce one ounce of gold. Lower AISC = wider profit margins when gold prices rise.

| Company | AISC (per oz) | Gold Price | Margin |
|---------|---------------|------------|--------|
| Company A | $1,050 | $2,000 | $950 |
| Company B | $1,350 | $2,000 | $650 |

#### 2. Price-to-Net Asset Value (P/NAV)

The P/NAV ratio compares a company's market capitalisation to the net present value of its mining assets. A P/NAV below 1.0 suggests the market is undervaluing the company's reserves.

#### 3. Reserve Life

How many years of gold production does the company have left? Longer reserve life provides more visibility into future cash flows.

## Comparing Gold Mining Stocks

When building a gold equity position, consider diversifying across:

- **Senior producers** — Large, stable companies with multiple mines
- **Junior miners** — Higher risk, higher potential reward
- **Royalty companies** — Asset-light model with exposure to gold prices

> "The best gold equity investment isn't always the cheapest — it's the one with the most sustainable margin of safety."

### Risk Factors to Watch

1. **Geopolitical risk** — Where are the mines located?
2. **Management quality** — Track record of capital allocation
3. **Balance sheet strength** — Debt-to-equity ratio
4. **Production growth** — Is output increasing or declining?

Understanding these fundamentals transforms gold equity investing from speculation into informed analysis.
